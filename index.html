    Emerging IT Technologies

    Clouds, Services, Servers
    What does it do?
    Cloud computing has revolutionised the way businesses and individuals manage and process their data. Cloud computing has paved the way for scalable, efficient, flexible and cost effective enterprise computing environments (Thecodest, 2023). The technology has and continues to rapidly develop over the years, offering a plethora of services and enabling more computing possibilities. At their core, clouds are large, interconnected servers that are accessible through the internet (Cloudflare, 2023). They allow users to access a shared pool of resources. Clouds provide on-demand access to resources, eliminating the need for local infrastructure and reducing setup costs dramatically. Cloud computing offers scalability which allows businesses to expand their computing resources on demand as needed.

    There are a range of different offerings that are available from cloud computing, including Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Software as a Service (SaaS). IaaS provides virtual computing resources which allow users to provision and manage virtual machines, storage servers and networks. PaaS allows developers to build, test and deploy applications in a scalable environment. SaaS houses ready to use software that can easily be setup and deployed (Watts & Raza, 2019).

    There are a number of large scale cloud providers such as Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud Platform who each have infrastructure and data centres located around the globe. Between all these providers, they offer a variety of services from storage and virtual machines to advanced analytics and artificial intelligence capabilities.

    Today, cloud computing allows organisations to leverage data analytics, machine learning and artificial intelligence to gain insights on their products. The benefits of doing this on the cloud means that businesses can process large volumes of data quickly, which allows them to be more productive. The cloud also allows for seamless collaboration with teams allowing for remote teams to work and access shared resources from anywhere in the world with an internet connection.

    Over the next couple of years, there are several advancements in cloud computing that are expected to revolutionise it. Cloud computing providers are expected to increase their scalability even more, allowing businesses to seamlessly scale their computing resources on demand.

    Cloud based Advance AI and Machine Learning services will become even more sophisticated and faster, allowing organisations to leverage powerful algorithms and predictive insights (7MA, 2021). This will help in various sectors including medicine, autonomous vehicles and automation. As technology like Quantum computing develops, cloud providers will be sure to integrate its capabilities into their services.

    Over the next few years, we will see more organisations starting to adopt a hybrid cloud model, where they are able to utilise the benefits of cloud computing whilst also having their important data stored locally for quicker access.

    Cloud computing will also play a vital role in promoting sustainability as providers continue to invest in energy efficient infrastructure, renewable energy sources and carbon offset initiatives to minimize their environmental impact (Beardmore, 2022). By also enabling organisations to allow remote work, they also reduce the environmental impacts from needing to commute.

    Cloud computing has transformed the way in which businesses and individuals handle their data, offering on demand scalability, flexible and cost efficient computing environments. Cloud computing has and continues to become a integral part of modern technology.

    Blockchain and Cryptocurrency
    What does it do?
    The first cryptocurrency was Bitcoin, which was created by either a programmer or a group of programmers using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. The identity of the creator of Bitcoin is still to this day unknown (Pinkerton 2023). Cryptocurrency is a digital currency which can’t be materialised and uses decentralised control. This means that transactions are strictly between the parties involved, there are no intermediaries facilitating the transactions such as banks like the normal monetary system and parties remain completely anonymous. This allows users to buy and sell things with little to no fees and is the main reason why interest in crypto is growing every year (Hassani et al. 2018).

    For these transactions to function and maintain security, a blockchain is used. A blockchain is basically a digital storage of transactions. These transactions are replicated and sent to a network of nodes to record all the information, verify it, and then create a block which is added to the chain. A block in a blockchain stores a certain number of transactions, then another block is created and distributed to all participants in the node network (Duggan 2022). Each transaction must be verified through digital signatures from each party involved in the transaction. These signatures are in the form of private and public keys. The keys are unique identifiers for each party which acts like a password and once each party has entered their keys the transaction is sent to all verification nodes.

    These nodes are operated by people that have been incentivised to verify transactions with the potential for rewards, they are known as crypto miners (Duggan 2022). Crypto mining is where you get specialised computers to conduct calculation which are used to record and verify every new crypto transaction. Mining is essentially a race between all participants. Each time a transaction occurs it is sent to nodes for verification. To verify the transactions, you need to guess a hash, which is a 64-digit hexadecimal number. Whoever guesses the number first updates the blockchain with a new block containing the newly verified transactions and in return they also receive a bitcoin or a unit of the cryptocurrency they are mining (Coinbase n.d). As the blockchain is shared between all nodes in the network, it makes it hard for fraudulent activity to occur because everyone can see the transaction, and everyone is trying to verify it.

    The spike in crypto popularity has given rise to new lending, borrowing and trading opportunities known as DeFi. DeFi or decentralized finance offers financial services much like banks but in the form of cryptocurrency. This is becoming more popular because it often reduces the fees and commissions you would otherwise pay if you went through a bank (Siripurapu and Berman 2023). The popularity of cryptocurrency has produced growing concerns within governments as the currency has no regulations. With no regulations the door to criminal activity is wide open with no way to track transactions to the individuals.

    The federal reserve bank is considering making their own form of digital currency that acts very similar to cryptocurrency with an attempt to add some regulation to reduce risks (Siripurapu and Berman 2023). The problem with this idea is that the popularity comes from the fact that cryptocurrency is decentralised (Hassani et al. 2018). So, a form of digital currency produced by the centralised bank may not be welcomed by already users of crypto. Cryptocurrencies are also increasing in acceptance by the retail industry, with more retailers accepting crypto as payment for goods and services (Finance Magnates 2023). If the trend continues it is going to put more pressure on existing financial institutions to come up with a response or risk being pushed out of the market.

    What is the likely impact?
    Some impacts of the rise of crypto are that it has created a platform for criminal activity such as ransomware attacks, which involve shutting down networks and demanding payment to restore them and drug cartels laundering money through virtual currencies, utilising the anonymous nature of the transactions. The reaction to these activities by the US government is to monitor the transactions of crypto back to the US dollar (Siripurapu and Berman 2023). This negative impact will see the need for authorities to find new ways of being able to regulate the use of cryptocurrencies.

    Another impact is user error. While it's said that blockchains are impossible to hack, your crypto wallet is only as safe as your seed phrases. Seed phrases are generated by your crypto wallet and then are used to derive your private keys which allow you to make a transaction (Duggan 2022). If someone was to acquire these phrases, for example, if you write them down and someone finds it, then that person will be able to use those phrases to steal all your currency. If you were to forget these phrases, then you would not be able to access your crypto and it is all essentially worthless. Wallets can also be “cold” which is the closest you can get to physically holding the currency. This involves depositing your crypto into a physical hardware device which is then turned off when you are not using it (Duggan 2022). If something were to happen to this cold wallet, then all the currency inside is lost.

    In contrast to your standard wallet with your bank card, if you lose your bank card, you can contact your financial institute report it stolen and have a new card delivered within a week. In my personal experience when money has been stolen out of my bank account, my financial institute has refunded the amount in full. If someone steals your wallet, has your passwords and spends your currency, there is no way of recovering any of it. With these points in mind the people that will be impacted firstly are households and businesses looking to loan, lend or invest money. With the growth in DeFi it is likely that more people will transition from regular financial institutions for the cheaper fees and commissions. The financial institutions themselves will also be impacted, if they lose more customers then they may cut employees due to lower demand for services. Finally, law enforcement will be impacted with a growing need for more eyes on the crypto criminal element, so as potential jobs are lost in the financial sector, there would be potential job growth in law enforcement.

    Natural Language Processing & Chatterbots

    Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that primarily encompasses software that enables computers to understand text and speech like the way humans do (IBM, n.d.). While NLP is a subset of Artificial Intelligence, the applications for Natural Language Processing are almost infinite – with any task involving the parsing of text or speech by a human and then acting on that information having the potential to be completed by using Natural Language processing techniques. As Natural Language Processing is a wide and complex field, this report will be focusing on text-based NLP.

    Natural Language Processing has roots in linguistics, with the understanding of context and nuances of language being the focus. Early uses of NLP were rule based, with systems and solutions fitted to specific applications. One of the earliest rule-based approaches to NLP was IBM’s Georgetown experiment, where a typist entered phrases in Russian into a computer and had the computer translate these back in English. (IBM, 1954). This was made possible by assigning rule tags to words in order to signal to the computer that a specific action needed to be applied, this could include reversing words, removing words that aren’t necessary and inserting new words. As an example, a rule tag 21 was used to signal to the computer that the Russian words “gyeneral major” should be reversed to “major general” in English. (IBM, 1954).

    As rule-based translation requires significant work by hand to understand language and craft rulesets, advances in computing technology unlocked newer methods of translation, particularly with Statistical Machine Translation (SMT). SMT shifted from relying on hand crafted rulesets to leveraging large texts and statistical models to generate probability based translations. IBM’s Candide system for translating French to English is one example of applying statistical methods to translate from one language to another. The model for Candide is comprised of ingesting English and French sentence pairs from the Canadian Hansard, an English-French record of the Canadian Parliament (Berger, et al. 1994).

    While statistical machine translation performs well when there is corpus available for the language pairs, it only works for specific pairs of languages such as the above example with English and French while less common language pairs have less reference data available, making statistical models hard to generate. Fortunately, further developments in Artificial Intelligence and particularly the development of the Transformer architecture for neural networks have had a major impact on the field of Natural Language Processing. The Transformer architecture is multiple layers of neural network mechanisms and components that work together to process an input and allow the model to contextualise the input it has received, allowing it to further recognize patterns and relationships in data. (Kulshresthra, 2020).

    Recently, developments in Natural Language Processing leveraging the Transformer architecture have been made available to the public with immediate impact. OpenAI’s GPT-3 (Generative Pre Trained Transformer 3) is a Large-Language-Model that has been trained on extensive and diverse datasets that amass to around 45TB of data and 175 billion parameters (Cooper, 2021). This has resulted in a chat-based product, Chat GPT - that is able to understand and generate text like a human would. A user of Chat GPT can provide the software with prompts containing contextual information, reference data or direction on what the user wants Chat GPT to do. It can, for example, take in a large text reference and summarise it for a user. It can also generate code in multiple programming languages. The implications of this technology are widespread, transformative, and potentially, completely unknown. (Mollick, 2022).

    Almost immediately, the impacts of releasing Chat GPT to the public were causing both tremendous excitement and concern for the future. The Education sector was one of the first industries to be affected by this emerging technology with schools having to rapidly learn both how their students were using this technology and how they can shape learning in the future to be enhanced by it (Roose, 2023). Professionals have also found success with using Chat GPT to automate their day-to-day tasks such as analysing data, responding to emails and creating content for their work.

    With all the success of time and effort saved by automating day-to-day tasks, the negatives of this emerging technology are starting to become visible with the fear of jobs being replaced by AI becoming a closer threat. IBM recently paused hiring in back-office type roles such as human resources as they expect with the improvements to the technology over time, AI could replace over 7000 employees (Edwards, 2023). As more people are aware of the potential dangers of more widespread AI adoption, criticism over who should be allowed to develop and control these models has become a hot topic recently. The European Union have recently called for regulation, with their AI Act aiming to ensure safety and transparency in artificial intelligence technical, and to prevent harmful outcomes ensuring that systems are overseen by humans. (European Parliament, 2023).

    Personally, I have used Chat GPT to look for a bug in code that I’ve written or explain why a unit test isn’t working and provide suggestions on how to fix it - this has saved me countless hours of frustration, enabling me to be more efficient in my day-to-day work. I also see a lot of opportunities for this technology to enhance the working lives of many people close to me - my partner who spends a lot of their time as a lawyer reviewing documents and case files could use this technology to summarise these documents for them, providing an improved work life balance. I have witnessed new folks in the software development industry using Chat GPT as a pair programmer to help them understand new languages and frameworks quicker.

    In March 2023 Open AI released their latest model, GPT 4 - which reported has 1 trillion parameters up from the 175 billion parameters which GPT 3 was trained on (Albergotti, 2023). As Chat GPT is currently using GPT 3.5, it is hard to predict what impact this new model will have on the world. Personally, I am concerned what the future holds for my profession and what Natural Language Processing and Large-Language Models such as GPT have on it. I hope that this technology enhances output and unlocks a more productive society, and not make software development by humans redundant like the calculator did to human calculators.

Once we established our meeting schedule and started allocating our work, the team was able to complete individual tasks assigned and show imitative to ensure the project was progressing.

We have all had past experiences of group projects where only one or two members are pulling all the weight, but in this project, everyone had an equal share and were bought into the success of the project.

    Lessons learned from this project include the importance of communication, especially when everyone is on a different schedule and has their own life commitments outside of schooling.
