Cybersecurity

When computers were first invented in the mid-1900s cyber-crime began to become popular almost immediately. However, the history of cybersecurity dates back even further. In 1934, a financial market attack in France targeted the telegraph system, showing the early beginnings of cyber threats (Arctic Wolf, 2022).

As cybercrimes were escalating, the need for robust defence mechanisms became obvious. In the 1970s, Ray Tomlinson the inventor of email, developed an innovative solution to fight against tracing worms known as “Creepers.” He created a program called “Reaper”, which hunted down and destroyed Creepers (Davies, 2021). Since then, cybersecurity measures have evolved in tandem with emerging cyber-attack methods and technologies in a constant race to keep up.

In recent years, the world has experienced a significant shift in the way we work due to the pandemic caused by COVID-19. The need to adapt to remote work arrangements has really opened up new opportunities for cyber-attacks targeting this type of work. With an estimated 48% of knowledge workers expected to be working in either hybrid or fully remote settings by 2023 (McLellan, 2023) the chance of cyber-attacks will only continue to grow. This reality pushes the envelope with the need to stay up to date with the latest technologies to prevent and/or mitigate these threats effectively.

As we look towards a future where we could potentially have new attacks prevented before they are developed, the integration of AI and machine learning technologies have shown to be a real game-changer in cybersecurity. By leveraging these technologies, companies can analyse a vast amount of data to identify trends, patterns and abnormalities that could indicate a cyber threat. AI and machine learning have the ability to process and interpret data in real-time, enabling us to detect cyber-attacks proactively and prevent them.

One notable technology in this field is IBM’s QRadar. Although it has been available since 2022, this technology is rapidly advancing due to the continuous development of AI. The system can collect, process and store network data in real time, which allows companies to manage their network security more efficiently. By providing real-time information and alerts about possible network threats, QRadar can enable companies and organizations to respond promptly and effectively (IBM, 2023).

The current state of AI and machine learning with regards to cybersecurity shows a real leap forward in threat detection and response. These technologies are able to detect known as well as unknown threats, which helps adapt to different emerging attacks and continuously learn new data. In the next three years, as we have seen new AI take leaps forward in recent years with the introduction of ChatGPT in November 2022 (Dheda, 2023). We could also be seeing leaps forward in these technologies alongside it, the field is definitely an exciting one with constant advancements being made. Additionally, along with increases in AI we are seeing computing power grow and grow, which also increases the availability to scale datasets and analyse even more data. These things will increase the learning potential of AI and help it to identify and counter new cyber threats earlier and earlier.

Cybersecurity being integrated with AI and machine learning can be viewed as ready to have a real transformative impact. From automating threat detections and responses, we can use these technologies to increase the accuracy of cybersecurity operations as well as increase the availability of high-quality cybersecurity services to companies. As of now companies can expect to spend quite a large budget on their cybersecurity departments with Facebook admittedly having spent $13 billion on “safety and security” since 2016, and having 40,000 staff dedicated to the field (Robertson, 2021). If we are able to get to a point where AI is able to automatically deal with threats as well as heighten overall protection against new threats, we can expect to see a significant drop in these numbers and an increase in availability to companies that might otherwise be running with a low budget for this field. Additionally, with a widespread implementation of these technologies it would create new roles and increase demand for skilled professionals who are able to optimize the use of these technologies.

Blockchain and Cryptocurrency

The first cryptocurrency was Bitcoin, which was created by either a programmer or a group of programmers using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. The identity of the creator of Bitcoin is still to this day unknown (Pinkerton 2023).

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency which can’t be materialised and uses decentralised control. This means that transactions are strictly between the parties involved, there are no intermediaries facilitating the transactions such as banks like the normal monetary system and parties remain completely anonymous. This allows users to buy and sell things with little to no fees and is the main reason why interest in crypto is growing every year (Hassani et al. 2018).

For these transactions to function and maintain security, a blockchain is used. A blockchain is basically a digital storage of transactions. These transactions are replicated and sent to a network of nodes to record all the information, verify it, and then create a block which is added to the chain. A block in a blockchain stores a certain number of transactions, then another block is created and distributed to all participants in the node network (Duggan 2022). Each transaction must be verified through digital signatures from each party involved in the transaction. These signatures are in the form of private and public keys. The keys are unique identifiers for each party which acts like a password and once each party has entered their keys the transaction is sent to all verification nodes. These nodes are operated by people that have been incentivised to verify transactions with the potential for rewards, they are known as crypto miners (Duggan 2022).

The spike in crypto popularity has given rise to new lending, borrowing and trading opportunities known as DeFi. DeFi or decentralized finance offers financial services much like banks but in the form of cryptocurrency. This is becoming more popular because it often reduces the fees and commissions you would otherwise pay if you went through a bank (Siripurapu and Berman 2023).

Some impacts of the rise of crypto are that it has created a platform for criminal activity such as ransomware attacks, which involve shutting down networks and demanding payment to restore them and drug cartels laundering money through virtual currencies, utilising the anonymous nature of the transactions. The reaction to these activities by the US government is to monitor the transactions of crypto back to the US dollar (Siripurapu and Berman 2023).

Natural Language Processing & Chatterbots

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that primarily encompasses software that enables computers to understand text and speech like the way humans do (IBM, n.d.). While NLP is a subset of Artificial Intelligence, the applications for Natural Language Processing are almost infinite – with any task involving the parsing of text or speech by a human and then acting on that information having the potential to be completed by using Natural Language processing techniques. As Natural Language Processing is a wide and complex field, this report will be focusing on text-based NLP.

Natural Language Processing has roots in linguistics, with the understanding of context and nuances of language being the focus. Early uses of NLP were rule based, with systems and solutions fitted to specific applications. One of the earliest rule-based approaches to NLP was IBM’s Georgetown experiment, where a typist entered phrases in Russian into a computer and had the computer translate these back in English. (IBM, 1954).

As rule-based translation requires significant work by hand to understand language and craft rulesets, advances in computing technology unlocked newer methods of translation, particularly with Statistical Machine Translation (SMT). SMT shifted from relying on hand crafted rulesets to leveraging large texts and statistical models to generate probability based translations. IBM’s Candide system for translating French to English is one example of applying statistical methods to translate from one language to another. The model for Candide is comprised of ingesting English and French sentence pairs from the Canadian Hansard, an English-French record of the Canadian Parliament (Berger, et al. 1994).

While statistical machine translation performs well when there is corpus available for the language pairs, it only works for specific pairs of languages such as the above example with English and French while less common language pairs have less reference data available, making statistical models hard to generate. Fortunately, further developments in Artificial Intelligence and particularly the development of the Transformer architecture for neural networks have had a major impact on the field of Natural Language Processing. The Transformer architecture is multiple layers of neural network mechanisms and components that work together to process an input and allow the model to contextualise the input it has received, allowing it to further recognize patterns and relationships in data. (Kulshresthra, 2020).

Recently, developments in Natural Language Processing leveraging the Transformer architecture have been made available to the public with immediate impact. OpenAI’s GPT-3 (Generative Pre Trained Transformer 3) is a Large-Language-Model that has been trained on extensive and diverse datasets that amass to around 45TB of data and 175 billion parameters (Cooper, 2021). This has resulted in a chat-based product, Chat GPT - that is able to understand and generate text like a human would. A user of Chat GPT can provide the software with prompts containing contextual information, reference data or direction on what the user wants Chat GPT to do. It can, for example, take in a large text reference and summarise it for a user. It can also generate code in multiple programming languages. The implications of this technology are widespread, transformative, and potentially, completely unknown. (Mollick, 2022).

Almost immediately, the impacts of releasing Chat GPT to the public were causing both tremendous excitement and concern for the future. The Education sector was one of the first industries to be affected by this emerging technology with schools having to rapidly learn both how their students were using this technology and how they can shape learning in the future to be enhanced by it (Roose, 2023). Professionals have also found success with using Chat GPT to automate their day-to-day tasks such as analysing data, responding to emails and creating content for their work.

With all the success of time and effort saved by automating day-to-day tasks, the negatives of this emerging technology are starting to become visible with the fear of jobs being replaced by AI becoming a closer threat. IBM recently paused hiring in back-office type roles such as human resources as they expect with the improvements to the technology over time, AI could replace over 7000 employees (Edwards, 2023). As more people are aware of the potential dangers of more widespread AI adoption, criticism over who should be allowed to develop and control these models has become a hot topic recently. The European Union has recently called for regulation, with their AI Act aiming to ensure safety and transparency in artificial intelligence technical, and to prevent harmful outcomes ensuring that systems are overseen by humans (European Parliament, 2023).

Personally, I have used Chat GPT to look for a bug in code that I’ve written or explain why a unit test isn’t working and provide suggestions on how to fix it - this has saved me countless hours of frustration, enabling me to be more efficient in my day-to-day work. I also see a lot of opportunities for this technology to enhance the working lives of many people close to me - my partner who spends a lot of their time as a lawyer reviewing documents and case files could use this technology to summarise these documents for them, providing an improved work life balance. I have witnessed new folks in the software development industry using Chat GPT as a pair programmer to help them understand new languages and frameworks quicker.

Cloud computing has revolutionised the way businesses and individuals manage and process their data. Cloud computing has paved the way for scalable, efficient, flexible and cost effective enterprise computing environments (Thecodest, 2023). The technology has and continues to rapidly develop over the years, offering a plethora of services and enabling more computing possibilities. At their core, clouds are large, interconnected servers that are accessible through the internet (Cloudflare, 2023). They allow users to access a shared pool of resources. Clouds provide on-demand access to resources, eliminating the need for local infrastructure and reducing setup costs dramatically. Cloud computing offers scalability which allows businesses to expand their computing resources on demand.

There are a range of different offerings that are available from cloud computing, including Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Software as a Service (SaaS). IaaS provides virtual computing resources which allow users to provision and manage virtual machines, storage servers and networks. PaaS allows developers to build, test and deploy applications in a scalable environment. SaaS houses ready to use software that can easily be setup and deployed (Watts & Raza, 2019).

There are a number of large scale cloud providers such as Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud Platform who each have infrastructure and data centres located around the globe. Between all these providers, they offer a variety of services from storage and virtual machines to advanced analytics and artificial intelligence capabilities.

Today, cloud computing allows organisations to leverage data analytics, machine learning and artificial intelligence to gain insights on their products. The benefits of doing this on the cloud means that businesses can process large volumes of data quickly, which allows them to be more productive. The cloud also allows for seamless collaboration with teams allowing for remote teams to work and access shared resources from anywhere in the world with an internet connection.

Over the next couple of years, there are several advancements in cloud computing that are expected to revolutionise it. Cloud computing providers are expected to increase their scalability even more, allowing businesses to seamlessly scale their computing resources on demand.

Cloud based Advance AI and Machine Learning services will become even more sophisticated and faster, allowing organisations to leverage powerful algorithms and predictive insights (7MA, 2021). This will help in various sectors including medicine, autonomous vehicles and automation. As technology like Quantum computing develops, cloud providers will be sure to integrate its capabilities into their services.

Over the next few years, we will see more organisations starting to adopt a hybrid cloud model, where they are able to utilise the benefits of cloud computing whilst also having their important data stored locally for quicker access.

Cloud computing will also play a vital role in promoting sustainability as providers continue to invest in energy efficient infrastructure, renewable energy sources and carbon offset initiatives to minimize their environmental impact (Beardmore, 2022). By also enabling organisations to allow remote work, they also reduce the environmental impacts from needing to commute.

Cloud computing has transformed the way in which businesses and individuals handle their data, offering on demand scalability, flexible and cost efficient computing environments. Cloud computing has and continues to become an integral part of modern technology.

Cloud computing continues to have an impact on the technology landscape, continuously changing the way businesses and individuals manage and utilize computing resources. It eliminates the need for companies to continue to have on site infrastructure. The cloud also allows for seamless remote working environments as it provides centralized access to applications, data and resources. This will allow for businesses to shift from needing employees to come into a physical office to allowing remote workers located anywhere in the world to work on projects.

In the coming years, traditional on site IT departments will start to become obsolete, with no need for companies to have a dedicated team managing the on site infrastructure as it will predominantly be cloud based (Solutions, 2022). Instead of managing an organisations hardware and software internally, companies will be able to outsource these tasks to cloud service providers making on site IT techs redundant. While Cloud Computing will take away some jobs in IT, it will also create new cloud based ones as all the cloud equipment will need to be replaced.

Advancements in cloud computing will also replace a number of jobs due to the streamlined and seamless automation capabilities that it can provide. Self driving vehicles and drones will inevitably replace the taxi and delivery industries, while AI will replace financial planners and accountants making them redundant.

Cloud computing enables me to access my own and my teams data and applications from anywhere in the world with an internet connection, meaning that both me and my team have the convenience of working remotely as we please. Sharing files with my clients is also very easy as all the data is already accessible through the internet and I only need to grant them access (there is no waiting for uploads). Group Project Idea

Introduction

In modern times, technology has been integrated into virtually every aspect of life, including meal planning and grocery shopping. Through the development of innovative applications, meal planning and shopping have been streamlined to meet the needs of the fast-paced lifestyle that characterizes modern society. The project idea proposed, Dinnerizer, seeks to enhance this experience by incorporating novel features into a meal planning application, providing a comprehensive solution to meal planning and grocery shopping.

Overview of the Project

"Dinnerizer" is a dynamic meal planning solution designed for both desktop and mobile platforms. The application presents a wide variety of dinner options tailored to the user's preferences, dietary restrictions, desired cuisine styles, and specific ingredients to aid in reducing food waste. Dinnerizer not only generates comprehensive recipes but also compiles an organized shopping list for user convenience. The overarching aim is to ease daily dinner decision-making, promote diversity in meals, minimize waste, and enhance the overall cooking experience for a broad range of users, including those with disabilities.

Motivation and Objectives

Dinnerizer stems from the common, everyday challenge of deciding what to cook. This daily issue often leads to repetitive meals, unhealthy takeaway options, and food wastage. By providing varied meal suggestions and a meticulously compiled shopping list, Dinnerizer promotes home cooking and healthier dietary habits. The main objective is to create a valuable tool that improves lifestyle by making the process of meal planning more efficient, enjoyable, and accessible for all users, irrespective of their dietary preferences or physical abilities.

Project Description

Dinnerizer is a comprehensive application with an extensive recipe database managed using SQLite for improved scalability and search functionality. The user experience is tailored for simplicity, with a keen focus on accessibility, accommodating users with visual impairments or other disabilities. The application implements a feature to include specific ingredients in the meal recommendations, aiming to help users reduce waste. With a continuous feedback loop with users, the application will be iteratively improved based on users' needs and suggestions, making it a truly user-centric solution.

Tools and Technologies Used

The Dinnerizer project will utilize a range of tools and technologies to create a robust, user-friendly application compatible with both desktop and mobile platforms. The core of the application will be developed in Java due to its extensive libraries, excellent security features, and its inherent cross-platform capabilities, making it ideal for building an application that needs to function seamlessly across various platforms.

For the graphical user interface, we plan to use JavaFX, known for its versatility and the richness of its controls, which will enable us to design an intuitive and visually appealing interface. Our aim is to make Dinnerizer a pleasing experience for users, both functionally and aesthetically.

An important aspect of the Dinnerizer application is data management, for which we will utilize SQLite. SQLite is a lightweight database solution known for its efficiency and portability, making it an ideal choice for an application that needs to function well on mobile devices.

The mobile component of Dinnerizer will be handled using the cross-platform capabilities of Java combined with mobile-specific libraries and frameworks. For Android, we will use Android Studio, Google's official IDE for Android development, which provides a comprehensive set of tools for building apps on every type of Android device. For iOS, we will explore the use of multi-platform frameworks such as React Native or Flutter, both renowned for their ability to create native-like mobile applications using a single codebase.

Skills Required

The development of Dinnerizer requires a team skilled in Java programming and experienced with JavaFX for GUI design. Knowledge of SQLite for efficient data management is essential. The team should also possess a sound understanding of user interface design principles, including accessibility features. The ability to conduct user research and usability testing, respond to feedback, and iteratively improve the application will be crucial to its successful development and user reception.

Projected Outcomes

Upon completion, Dinnerizer will serve as a valuable tool that simplifies meal planning and offers a structured approach to grocery shopping. By alleviating decision fatigue, Dinnerizer will facilitate a diverse range of meals, promoting healthier home-cooked diets. Efficient grocery shopping could also mean less waste and cost-saving. As a future-oriented application, it will include plans for user feedback, potential monetisation considerations, and marketing plans to ensure that the application is continuously improved and relevant to its users.

Innovation and Uniqueness

The Dinnerizer application is set apart by its commitment to reducing waste by suggesting meals based on specific ingredients and promoting varied and diverse meals. Its dedication to accessibility ensures its usability across a wide demographic, including visually impaired and physically disabled individuals. The inclusion of a continuous feedback loop and the use of lightweight databases for improved scalability and search functionality adds a layer of innovation to this project. Dinnerizer goes beyond a conventional meal planning application by offering a comprehensive solution tailored to the user's preferences, dietary restrictions, desired cuisine styles, and specific ingredients.

Feasibility and Market Needs

As of the current technology landscape, Dinnerizer is a feasible project that aligns well with the digital trends in the food and lifestyle sector. Given the increased awareness about sustainable living and healthy eating, the market demand for such an application is high. Moreover, the tools and technologies needed for the project are widely available, and the skills required align with standard software development skill sets.

Feedback Incorporation and Detailed Mechanics

The idea of Dinnerizer came about from one of the group members' ideas and the feedback received from our Assignment. The suggestion to make it more inclusive and accessible came directly from feedback as it emphasized the importance of software accessibility in today's world. This led us to consider including features that make our application usable by individuals with disabilities. Based on the feedback received, we have planned to include a feature that lets users include specific ingredients in their meal recommendations to help reduce food waste.

Conclusion

The Dinnerizer project aspires to bring about a revolution in the field of meal planning and grocery shopping. With its focus on accessibility, diversity in meals, reduction in food waste, and comprehensive shopping lists, it is more than just a meal planning app. It's a tool designed to enhance lifestyle, promote healthier habits, and bring joy back to home cooking. The development of this application will contribute to the tech industry by addressing a common problem with a holistic solution that puts users and their needs at the forefront.
