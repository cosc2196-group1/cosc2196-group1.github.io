UI/UX Developer
Demand:
Based on the data from industry.gov.au, labourmarketinsights.gov.au, and Burning Glass, there is a growing demand for UI/UX Developers. It is expected to increase from 64,100 jobs in 2021 to 78,100 jobs in 2026. Over 70% of workers in this field work full-time, indicating both the robust demand for the role and the viability of the job as the primary source of income.

Required skills:
The average age of a UI/UX Developer is 35, and over 85% hold a degree in related fields. Essential general skills include problem-solving, active listening, and critical thinking. Key IT-specific skills involve proficiency in design software like Sketch and Figma, as well as languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Labour Market Insights, 2023).

IT Work

Security Ops:
A day in the life of a cyber security analyst starts a lot like most jobs, checking emails for any priority tasks. Morning meetings are incorporated into the day to day to collaborate with the team and see how everyone is traveling with their tasks. The types of projects a cybersecurity analyst will work on varies, some may answer calls from existing clients and investigate problems/implement fixes. Other jobs can be working on benchmark security protocols specific to a project or client and make them available online. Throughout the day analysts will check the Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) which monitors the traffic on the network for any malicious activity. As an analyst continues to improve their skills, they can advance in their career to start working on firewalls and switching and routing. This is usually configuring the firewalls, switches and routers and optimising their effectiveness (Day In The Life Of A Cyber Security Analyst. Software Engineer

As uncovered in the industry insights section of this report, the role of a Software Engineer is incredibly varied depending on the company they work for, the industry they’re in and what type of software development they do. Typically, a Software Engineer works as an individual contributor, taking on multiple tasks they have either been assigned by their manager or through a central project management and collaboration software tool such as Jira or Trello. The types of tasks a developer might do include implementing feature requests, investigating and solving bugs in software, writing documentation, and designing and architecting new software systems.

While Software Engineers and Developers are the individuals writing code and implementing features and bug fixes, taking a software project from ideation to the end user is not single handedly performed by developers, with interactions by UI / UX Designers, Product Managers and Project Managers all common. The most common type of challenge experienced by people working as software developers is solving complex bugs that are hard to reproduce, or when a project is taking longer to complete than expected and having to spend some time working later than expected to meet the deadline. In some of the videos reviewed, interviewing candidates was often referenced in their day-to-day activities, affirming that there is a lot of demand from employers to hire people into these roles.

Many companies who employ software engineers have introduced flexible working arrangements including working from home or hybrid work. This means that software engineers and the other people they interact with often have the freedom to work from where they are most comfortable, with a mix of working from home and spending some time in the office for some of the week being anecdotally a common choice.

Network Engineer

Network Engineers play an important role in ensuring the network infrastructure is operating effectively within their organisation. They can work directly for an organisation, or they can work for a specialised service provider who works with multiple organisations. This can be a demanding and varied role, with network outages meaning their organisation or clients are unable to work effectively.

As with all IT roles, change is expected, and the role of a Network Engineer is no different. People within these roles are expected to stay up to date with the latest technology, trends, and advancements in the industry. Fortunately, vendors who provide networking products often have certification programs which engineers can take advantage of.

Unlike other IT roles, Network Engineers have a varied work location, some will work either from home or their office exclusively, while others will visit data centres and client offices to install and diagnose issues with networking hardware, often interacting with other technical professions within IT while doing so including System Administrators and Security Operations professionals.

Group Project Idea

Introduction

In modern times, technology has been integrated into virtually every aspect of life, including meal planning and grocery shopping. Through the development of innovative applications, meal planning and shopping have been streamlined to meet the needs of the fast-paced lifestyle that characterizes modern society. The project idea proposed, Dinnerizer, seeks to enhance this experience by incorporating novel features into a meal planning application, providing a comprehensive solution to meal planning and grocery shopping.

Overview of the Project

"Dinnerizer" is a dynamic meal planning solution designed for both desktop and mobile platforms. The application presents a wide variety of dinner options tailored to the user's preferences, dietary restrictions, desired cuisine styles, and specific ingredients to aid in reducing food waste. Dinnerizer not only generates comprehensive recipes but also compiles an organized shopping list for user convenience. The overarching aim is to ease daily dinner decision-making, promote diversity in meals, minimize waste, and enhance the overall cooking experience for a broad range of users, including those with disabilities.

Motivation and Objectives

Dinnerizer stems from the common, everyday challenge of deciding what to cook. This daily conundrum often leads to repetitive meals, unhealthy takeaway options, and food wastage. By providing varied meal suggestions and a meticulously compiled shopping list, Dinnerizer promotes home cooking and healthier dietary habits. The main objective is to create a valuable tool that improves lifestyle by making the process of meal planning more efficient, enjoyable, and accessible for all users, irrespective of their dietary preferences or physical abilities.

Project Description

Dinnerizer is a comprehensive application with an extensive recipe database managed using SQLite for improved scalability and search functionality. The user experience is tailored for simplicity, with a keen focus on accessibility, accommodating users with visual impairments or other disabilities. The application implements a feature to include specific ingredients in the meal recommendations, aiming to help users reduce waste. With a continuous feedback loop with users, the application will be iteratively improved based on users' needs and suggestions, making it a truly user-centric solution.

Tools and Technologies Used

Dinnerizer will leverage Java due to its wide-ranging libraries and cross-platform capabilities. JavaFX will be utilized for crafting a user-friendly interface, and SQLite will serve as the efficient data management solution. Java's built-in I/O libraries will be used for effective data manipulation. Other libraries may be explored to facilitate exporting shopping lists either as printable PDFs or directly sent to users' emails.

Skills Required

The development of Dinnerizer requires a team skilled in Java programming and experienced with JavaFX for GUI design. Knowledge of SQLite for efficient data management is essential. The team should also possess a sound understanding of user interface design principles, including accessibility features. The ability to conduct user research and usability testing, respond to feedback, and iteratively improve the application will be crucial to its successful development and user reception.

Projected Outcomes

Upon completion, Dinnerizer will serve as a valuable tool that simplifies meal planning and offers a structured approach to grocery shopping. By alleviating decision fatigue, Dinnerizer will facilitate a diverse range of meals, promoting healthier home-cooked diets. Efficient grocery shopping could also mean less waste and cost-saving. As a future-oriented application, it will include plans for user feedback, potential monetisation considerations, and marketing plans to ensure that the application is continuously improved and relevant to its users.

Innovation and Uniqueness

The Dinnerizer application is set apart by its commitment to reducing waste by suggesting meals based on specific ingredients and promoting varied and diverse meals. Its dedication to accessibility ensures its usability across a wide demographic, including visually impaired and physically disabled individuals.
